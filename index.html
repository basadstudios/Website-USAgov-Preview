United States Government

Understanding the structure and functions of the U.S. government

The Three Branches of Government

Executive Branch

Led by the President, this branch executes and enforces federal laws.

  • The President of the United States
  • Vice President
  • Cabinet and Executive Departments
  • Independent Agencies
  • Executive Office of the President
Learn More

Legislative Branch

Composed of Congress, this branch makes the nation's laws.

  • The Senate (100 members)
  • House of Representatives (435 members)
  • Legislative Agencies
  • Congressional Committees
Learn More

Judicial Branch

Evaluates laws and resolves disputes on their application.

  • Supreme Court
  • U.S. Courts of Appeals
  • U.S. District Courts
  • Specialized Courts
Learn More

Levels of Government

Federal Government

Manages national issues like defense, foreign policy, and interstate commerce.

State Government

Handles issues like education, transportation, and healthcare within each state.

Local Government

Manages city or county services like police, fire, utilities, and local transportation.

The U.S. Constitution

The supreme law of the United States, establishing the framework of the federal government and protecting citizens' rights.

  • Ratified in 1788
  • 7 Articles and 27 Amendments
  • Created federal government with checks and balances
Learn More

Bill of Rights

The first ten amendments to the Constitution that guarantee essential rights and liberties to American citizens.

  • Freedom of speech, religion, and press
  • Right to bear arms
  • Protection against unreasonable searches
  • Right to due process
Learn More

American Landmarks & Monuments

Explore the historical sites and iconic landmarks that represent America's heritage and values

Featured

Statue of Liberty

New York, NY

The Statue of Liberty, a gift from the people of France to the United States, has stood as a symbol of freedom and democracy since its dedication in 1886. Standing at 305 feet tall from ground to torch, the copper statue designed by Frédéric Auguste Bartholdi welcomes visitors to New York Harbor.

National Monument
UNESCO World Heritage
Est. 1886
Est. 1922

Lincoln Memorial

Washington, DC

A national monument built to honor Abraham Lincoln, the 16th President of the United States.

Est. 1941

Mount Rushmore

Keystone, SD

A sculpture carved into the granite face of Mount Rushmore featuring the heads of George Washington, Thomas Jefferson, Theodore Roosevelt, and Abraham Lincoln.

Est. 1800

The White House

Washington, DC

The official residence and workplace of the President of the United States, built between 1792 and 1800.

Est. 1937

Golden Gate Bridge

San Francisco, CA

An iconic suspension bridge spanning the Golden Gate, the one-mile-wide strait connecting San Francisco Bay and the Pacific Ocean.

Est. 1800

U.S. Capitol

Washington, DC

The meeting place of the United States Congress and the seat of the legislative branch of the U.S. federal government.
